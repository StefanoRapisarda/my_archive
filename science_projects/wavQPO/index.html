A wavelet look at the type-C/type-B QPO transition in MAXI J1820+070

Project Description

An amazing project description.

Dataset

The project focuses on the QPO type switch observed with NICER, so, naturally, the dataset consists of the same NICER data used in Homan et al. 2020 and HXMT observations. According to Homan et al. 2020 the transition happened on July 6 at 16:15UTC. It seems that HXMT just missed the transition... however there is data collected on the 6th and 8th of July.

Observations

  • NICER;
  • HXMT Obs. ID P0114661084 - prop. P0114661, 2018-07-06T20:41:14 - 2018-07-07T06:22:08, 34854 s;
  • HXMT Obs. ID P0114661085 - prop. P0114661, 2018-07-08T01:19:20 - 2018-07-08T09:24:39, 29119 s;

Previous Results

From [1]
  • The evolution of the outburst is broadly devided into three branches: low-hard state (MJD 58189-58290), high-soft state and transitions (MJD 58290-58400), and low-hard state again (MJD 58400-58443);
  • Before MJD 58195 emission seems to be affected by the presence of a warm absorber, this resulted in strong, spectrally hard, dips;
  • The first hard-soft transition seems to happen between MJD 58302 and MJD 58308 (2018 July 3-9);
  • In July 6-7 (so between July 6 and 9) a QPO transition (from C to B has been observed). At the beginning of this period Type-C QPOs have been originally observed at frequencies 4.5 and 9 Hz, respectively. The transition is specifically observed at MJD 58305.67740 (July 6 16:15 UTC). A type-B QPO was clearly observed at this time with frequency around 4.5 Hz. The type-B QPO was only present for five GTIs (4100s) during which it slightly decreased to about 3Hz. As in the transition the luminosity of the source increases, some of the detectors have been shut off because of telemetry problems. The combination of intrinsic weakining of the feature and reducing effective area, made subsequent detection of the Type-B QPO uncertain. Type-B QPO desappeared after MJD 58305.81136, July 6 19:28 UTC).
  • Fitting AMI-LA radio lightcurve between July 5 and 8 (MJD 55304.77201-58307.15169) with three components addressing respectively the baseline decay, the radio flare, and the radio flare decay, they managed to extrapolate the radio flare start time with high precition. They found a start time of MJD 58305.773 ± 0.006 (July 6 18:33 UTC, with a statistical uncertainty of about 10 minutes);
  • They found that the estimated flare start is about 2-2.5 hr later than the time at which the type-B QPO appeared;
  • The appearence of the type-B QPO coincided with the start of a small flare in the 7-12 keV energy band. The flare duration and the detection time interval of the Type-B QPO seem to be the same;

Introduction points

Reading [1]
  • This is a kind of unique dataset because it captured the moment at which a narrow Type-B QPO appeared around 4.5 Hz. In the previously studied sources this switch, o transition, between Type-C and Type-B QPOs always happened during gaps in the X-ray coverage;
  • They measured the transition with accuracy, but they could not do the same for the duration of the Type-B QPO. Perhaps this time can also give some hints about the relation betweem radio flares and Type-B QPO.

Discussion points

Reading [1]
  • One of the main goal of this project is (or became) getting a high precision measurement of the gap between QPO appearence and jet lauch. The physical meaning of this delay and its meaning in the context of jet models must be discussed. This is also to highlight the importance of methods to achieve such high precision measurement;
  • About why the frequency becomes lower, I can discuss the phenomenon in the context of the precessing flow model. For that, I would need to look for more existing information about the geometry of the flow during this transition, specifically about the truncation ratio;
  • It seems that the main competing models for Type-B (and perhaps C) QPOs are 1) precession model and 2) base of the jet model. I know basically nothing about the latter, but it seems that the simultaneous presence of hard X-ray flares (due to ejections) and Type-B QPO would strenghten the case for a jet-based origin of the Type-B QPO.
    On the other hand, the precession model would explain the Type-B QPO simply as Type-C QPO, but with a depleted inner disk. If this is true, we expect a decrease in variability at high energy (as pointed out in my paper in 2014). This could be a point of discussion (precession model vs. base of the jet model);

General considerations

Reading [1]
  • At the very beginning of my "wavelet adventure", the goal of applying the transform to QPO analysis was very ambitious, i.e. characterizing the morphology of the signal. After playing a bit with simulated signals, I've realized that this is particularly difficult when we are dealing with weak signals on top of broad band noise.
    However, reading Jeroen paper, I've realuized that wavelet analysis could provide a very precise measurements of minimum and maximum QPO frequency, introducing in this way a new tool to add high precision measurements to the current phenomenology.
    The new goal of this project is therefore measuring QPO frequency with high precision and monitoring these changes along state transitions.
  • This paper shows that with simple fitting it is possible to extrapolate the radio flare starting time with relatively high precision (statistical error of about 10 minutes). In the broad picture of investigating the connection between Type-B QPO and radio jets, it is fundamental to establish the transition or appearence of Type-B QPOs with comparable high precision. The wavelet transform could provide that;
  • The appearence of the Type-B QPO is simultaneous to drop in strength of broad band variability. I wonder how this variability would look in the wavelet transform;
  • A very important thing to consider for this kind of study is gaps. Ideally you want no-gaps data for both X-ray and radio sources, but as the paper shows you can still extrapolate the radio flare start time with fitting. Unfortunately you cannot do anything similar for the Type-C --> Type-B QPO transition. So, first thing to check, no gap data around the switch;
