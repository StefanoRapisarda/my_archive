Managing data/software

  • On the importance on data/software planning

    People expect that the most difficoult and costly phase in the lifecycle of a database/software development is the initial development: you need to figure out how to implement the features of your database/software, often all the feature you will need are not completely clear in your mind, you have to figure out how to implement them, and most probably you will have to phase a lot of new concepts. This is also the time when, more or less conssciously, you will take shortcuts as, quite understandably, you wish your software/database to work as soon as possible. Well, as a matter of fact, the hardest part comes later, during the maintenance phase. That is the phase where programmers/researchers pay the price for all the shortcuts they took during developments. It is indeed quite common for database/software to grow in complexity with time, a complexity they have not been initially designed for.
