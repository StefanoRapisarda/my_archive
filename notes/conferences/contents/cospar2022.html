To mention

  • To properly make prediction of the full timing properties (QPOs + noise), we need to fully understand the relation between disk and corona, how they talk to each other and what happens in different time scales;
  • The all story of the bending wave radius;
  • The viscous frequency determines both the characteristic frequency at which the fluctuation at a given radius is generated and the radial velocity through the flow. This corresponds to the physical picture that the variability faster than the viscous time-scales is attenuated through the diffusion and that the mass accretion happens in the viscous time-scales (Churazov et al. 2001; Ingram 2016) [1]
  • These works (for example mine and [1]) show that, despite we cannot yet obtain a precise prediction of the data with the propagating fluctuations models, we can make them run in geometrical scenarios that match results from spectral modelling. This could be of great help in discerning between conflicts of spectral models that fit equally well the data;
  • When talking about propagating fluctuation models it comes often out the word "linearity". The discussion about linearity is related to the question: how does the energy spectrum react when the mass accretion rate change? In a linear case, variations of the normalization of the energy spectrum at all energy and the mass accretion rate have the same sign and the same amplitude. However, reality can be different from the linear case. For example, we now that the power-law index of the Compton region depends on two parameters: electron temperature of the hot flow and optical depth. Mass accretion rate fluctuations may trigger variations that works on opposite side. Fluctuations propagating from the disc may cause variations in the seed photons, and so electron temperature of the hot flow region that is closest to the disk, and variations in the optical depths, causing a pivoting of the power law and so a non-linear response to propagating fluctuations.

  • The hard Comptonized continuum spectra seen in the low/hard state cannot arise from an isotropically emitting corona over the inner accretion disc (Poutanen et al. 2018) [1];

General considerations

  • In spectral analisys, the truncation radius is estimated mainly from observations of the broadening of the Iron line and from the normalization of the multi-temperature blackbody component;
  • Implementing mass accretion rate models using the Timmer & Koenig prescription is not appropriate for two main reasons: 1) In order to obtain the power spectrum you should average over many realization of the process, this means that a single "propagation" through the disk would be an incorrect prediction of the power spectrum; 2) The process is computationally expensive.
  • Model contraddiction and descrepancies between model and data seem to occur mainly during intermidiate states, when the energy spectrum may be composed of two Comptonization continua [3].
  • It seems that one of the main efforts in modelling, and also phenomenological studies, is trying to describe the accretion states observed in X-ray binaries in terms of few parameters, namely mass accretion rate (inderectly related to angular momentum transport and so magnetic field structure), black hole mass, black hole spin, and inclination;

Phenomenology

  • In the case of XTE J1752−223, Garcia et al. (2018) analysed very high signal to noise RXTE observations averaging a large number of "stable" (in terms of luminosity and hardness) hard state observations (~300 ks). The analysis led to a quite small inner disk radius measurement (1.7 ± 0.4 Rg). This is at odd with the truncated disc model. Furthermore they found that the data can almost equally well described with either an extended coronal model or a lampost geometry;

Models

  • [3] Truncated cold disc and hot accretion flow within its inner boundaries. The hot accretion flow radiates in the X-rays via proceeding Comptonization processes. The seed photons are coming from both the cold disc and the self-generated synchrotron emission. Mass accretion rate fluctuations are stirred up in the disk region closeset to the accretion flow and in the particle density of in the hot accretion flow. Because here we have both Compton up-scattering of seed photons and synchrotron photons coming from the flow itself, these variations lead to changes of the X-ray flux: first time, through the disc Comptonization in the region close to the disc truncation radius, and second time, through the synchrotron Comptonization in the region close to the black hole (within about 30–50 Schwarzschild radii). These two hard variable emissions may interfere with each either leading to complex power spectral shape. The main difference betweeh this scenario and the others, is that, instead of having a propagation region rigidly stratified, we have hot flow components significantly contributing to hard and soft band. Presence of two components in the light curves cause interference picture in their power and cross-spectra and oscillatory behaviour of the phase/time lags.
